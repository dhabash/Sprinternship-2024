VPOTUS made an unannounced stop in Concord, N.H. at 1:40 p.m., at Gibson's Bookstore & Cafe on S. Main Street.

She stepped out of her SUV and greeted a man and a woman at the front door: "Hi! Last time we were here together it was 20 degrees colder!"

Per the White House, the hosts are Gibson's owner Michael Herrmann and U.S. Rep. Annie Kuster.

VPOTUS made small talk with the owners in the middle of the retail sales floor against a backdrop of bookshelves. She made no news. Topics your pooler overheard included rural broadband, "child care deserts" and recipes.

As VPOTUS left the store and walked back to the SUV, she waved to a handful of well-wishers. Your pooler made eye contact and asked her if she had a plan to save Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs) that are struggling financially. She walked past and didn't respond.

Motorcade rolled at 1:56 p.m. for the workforce training event.

VPOTUS has not yet taken any questions today.
