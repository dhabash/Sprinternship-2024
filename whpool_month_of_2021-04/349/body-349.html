Highlights

*** Multiple speakers told Dr. Biden that her visiting Navajo Nation three times "must be some kind of record." She seemed visibly moved.


KEE ALLEN BEGAY (gave invocation and prayer in English and Navajo)

The Bless our Navajo people struggling with Covid 19.

We hope that our struggle is being heard in DC


CLARA PRATTE (emcee)

For the Navajo people we are nothing if not resilient.

There is not one of us who has not been affected by resounding loss.


PRESIDENT JONATHAN NEZ (Youngest Navajo Nation president ever)

Said there is a Navajo word, Joba’í, that sounds like “Joe Biden” and it means “compassion.” “That’s how a lot of our elders remembered it at the polls"

Thanked Jill Biden for "the magnification of our resilience" and "for making the Navajo Nation a priority in the first months of the Biden-Harris administration."

Said he met with President Biden when he was "Nominee Biden" and that he "understood the importance of government to government dialogue."

At the library, President Nez said he showed Dr. Biden the Navajo Nation's copy of the treaty of 1868

Highlighted how Navajo Nation came out to support the Bidens with "60, 70, even 80 percent turnout in some places" and that he is "pleased they are keeping their promise to keep a seat for us at the table."

Mourned the loss of 1,263 lives lost in Navajo Nation to the pandemic.


VICE PRESIDENT MYRON LIZER

Spoke about the need to preserve and conserve Navajo land and teach financial literacy.

Re: the kind of program needed to rebuild Navajo Nation: "President Nez called it Build Back Better. On Indian County I’ve called it the Great Catch-up."



CLARA PRATTE (again, she's the emcee, so popped up after every speaker."

To Dr. Biden: "You've visited Navajo Nation at least three times. That has to be a record."

"We need to get her her own personal hogan so she has a hogan to come back to." (She's referring to a traditional Navajo house) Everyone applauded.
"An Airbnb hogan!"

The commitment the Biden-Harris administration has to Indian Country -- this is not the norm."

Spoke about how "the distances here are vast" and there's a huge need for infrastructure and development.


SPEAKER SETH DAMON (introduced each member of the Navajo Council in turn. There may have been 20 introductions)

He said that even though it was cold, "I think this is one of those evenings Dr. Jill Biden will never forget."
Dr. Biden nodded vigorously.

"It must be a record for you to be here. I don’t think there’s ever been a dignitary of that level whose been here as many times."

"I think you come here because you want to learn more from the Navajo Nation." (Biden nodded)

Spoke about a colleague they'd lost on the Navajo council.

Called this "the woman nation" and told Dr. Biden "you're a fierce warrior."

Said he felt like they had "not only a leader and a friend, but a protector in the White House"

Spoke about murdered and Indigenous women

Introduce several council members wearing impressive black ranchers' hats. Introduced a woman from Sweetwater, Arizona, and told Dr. Biden that if she was going to vist her there, "you'll need more than a 4x4 truck" because the roads are difficult. Talked about rodeo and how Navajos are champions at it.

Introduced people who are working to revive the Navajo language

Re: Pendleton: "That was given to you by the Navajo Nation Council to protect yourself and keep you warm."

CHIEF JUSTICE JOANN JAYNE (Navajo Nation has three branches of government like ours and she's in charge of the judiciary)

She said millions are inspired by Dr. Biden's quote, "Teaching isn’t just what I do. It’s who I am."

"Thank you for being an advocate. This is who you are."

Navajo Nation just celebrated 62 years an independent judiciary

Called Dr. Biden "sister" in English and in Navajo.

Closed by saying, "May you walk in beauty."


FIRST LADY PHEFELIA NEZ AND SECOND LADY DOTTIE LIZER

(introduced Dr. Biden via her biography)


DR. JILL BIDEN (took off blanket to speak, was greeted with standing ovation)

"Oh, please sit down."

Talked about being grateful to have met "a group of women who I now call my sister warriors."

Was proud to be addressing Navajo Nation "on a day that we recommitted to protecting Mother Earth" -- referencing President Biden's address on Climate change.

Spoke about seeing pattern on a Navajo woven basket and thinking "that the world was created in perfect harmony." Praised the "adaptability, resilience, and strength of the Navajo people."

She gave a commencement address for the Navajo Technical College in 2013 and spoke about meeting "students working two jobs when they had no electricity in their homes," and others who "had to drop out out of necessity" and then "reenrolled and became honor students."

Spoke about the cancer center she helped dedicate and said, "it is the first of its kind, but it won't be the last." And it meant that people on Navajo Nation no longer have to drive hours to get lifesaving treatments.

Last year, President Nez joined in a virtual Indian talking circle.

"In our first 100 days, I am here to lest you know that we are keeping that promise. We stand with you in Indian Country."

Said President Biden had made the largest single investment in Indian Country.

Turned to talking about the pandemic. "This pandemic is so painful," and how many had died. "We feel their loss deeply every day." Praised efforts of President Nez to turn the numbers around, after having, in May, the worst covid rates per capita in the United States.

Now fifty percent of the population is vaccinated, and they've done it with "few resources," reaching people in remote areas. She called Navajo Nation "a leading example of the covid-19 response."

She talked about how the Biden administration had revived the White House Council of Indian Affairs and how President Biden in the jobs bill wants to improve Navajo Nation with running water, broadband, and electricity, that will create many jobs.

She says the administration wants to be "stewards of tribal land."

"Tribes will always have a voice in our administration."

She wants "to find the path back to Hozhó" (she pronounced it "hojo," which might be correct!) which roughly means "harmony"

With beauty before me, I walk
With beauty behind me, I walk
With beauty above and around me, I walk
Joe and I will walk with you, today, tomorrow, and always
(double check this)

She had a slower standing ovation, but everyone was freezing!
