Good afternoon from sunny Albuquerque. The pool overnighted here at Hotel Parq Central and Dr. Biden spent this morning virtually teaching. Yesterday's 4/21 visit was to a vaccination center, First Choice Community Healthcare, in Albuquerque's majority-Hispanic South Valley district.

The pool has been tested for covid-19 in preparation for the First Lady's two-day visit to Navajo Nation. This entire three-day trip to New Mexico and Arizona is her longest solo trip as first lady.

She'll be going by car from Albuquerque to Window Rock, Arizona, the tribal capital -- about a 2-hour, 45-minute drive northwest of Albuquerque and just west of the New Mexico border. Pool is spending the night in Gallup, NM, a thirty-minute drive back over the border.

The visit is meant to "highlight the Administration's commitment to standing with the Navajo Nation and all of Indian Country," according to her office.

Today there will be a welcoming ceremony at 5pm with President Jonathan Nez and other tribal leaders. She and First Lady Phefelia Nez will meet with Navajo women, then Dr. Biden will give a live radio address to Navajo Nation in front of the red sandstone arch after which the tribal capital is named. It's also the site of a veteran's memorial the tribe established. According to the timing, she may be giving the speech as the sun sets behind her, which should make for a dramatic setting.

Tomorrow morning (4/23) she will attend a listening session with students from across Navajo Nation at a boarding school, then will visit a vaccination site. After having the country's highest per-capita coronavirus rates, nearly half of the Navajo Nation population is vaccinated, far higher than the national average.

This is Dr. Biden's third trip to Navajo Nation, but her first as first lady. She last went in 2019 to celebrate the opening of a cancer center on the western side of the reservation. Her first visit was in 2013 to deliver the commencement address to Navajo Technical University. She has a longstanding relationship with President Jonathan Nez.

BACKGROUND FROM FIRST LADY'S OFFICE

Today, Thursday, April 22, the First Lady will begin her two-day visit to the Navajo Nation. She will highlight the Administration’s commitment to standing with the Navajo Nation and all of Indian Country.

Upon arrival to the Navajo Nation’s capital city, she will greet with Navajo Nation government leaders. Dr. Biden and First Lady Phefelia Nez will then greet with nine Navajo women.

Next, the First Lady will attend a welcome ceremony at the Window Rock Navajo Tribal Park & Veterans Memorial. Speaking in front of the historic red sandstone arch, the First Lady will deliver a live radio address to the Navajo Nation.

This will be the First Lady’s third visit to the Navajo Nation since 2013.
