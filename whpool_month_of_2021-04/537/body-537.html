Dr. Jill Biden came out onto the North Lawn at 10:28 am to plant a Linden tree in honor of Arbor Day. The tree replaces one removed last month that was deemed a risk. The older tree was not planted by an historical figure.

FLOTUS wore a blue and green dress with a blue blazer over it and blue heels.

“Who doesn’t plant trees in high heels?,” she said as she stepped up, shovel in hand. She added three shovels full of dirt.

She said the cat is coming “pretty soon” but is not at White House yet.

“No not yet,” she said.

Kym Hall, Regional Director, National Park Service, also added dirt.

The two shovels used are made of original White House wood and the blade is painted gold. They have plaques on them that say Laura Bush Jefferson Elm, Barack Obama Little leaf linden, and George W Bush Scarlett oak - per the eagle eyed photographers.

She left at 10:31 am.

It is 66 degrees and cloudy with a slight breeze.

Background from the East Wing:

The First Lady will participate in a tree planting event on the North Grounds of the White House to observe Arbor Day.

FLOTUS will be planting a Linden tree, selected by the National Park Service and sourced by Dale Haney, the White House Grounds Superintendent. The new Linden tree will replace one removed from the grounds last month after being identified as a risk during a routine assessment by the National Park Service.

  • Both the new tree, and removed tree, are Linden trees.
  • National Park Service experts identified the previous tree as a risk in October 2020 due to the hollowing and decay of the trunk, which was a natural result of its age and extended lifecycle.
  • The previous tree was not planted by a historical figure.
  • Two nearby trees of the same age remain (different species). These two historic trees are approximately 120 years old, from the same 1900’s landscaping period.
  • The ceremonial shovel FLOTUS will use is made of original White House wood, and has been used by several previous administrations.

FLOTUS will join members of the National Park Service and White House Executive Residence staff:

Kym Hall, Regional Director, National Park Service

John Stanwich, National Park Service Liaison to the White House

Katie Hinson, Deputy Chief Usher, The White House

Dale Haney, Grounds Superintendent, The White House

BIOGRAPHIES

Kym Hall, Regional Director, National Park Service

Kym Hall serves as Area Director for Region 1 – National Capital Area (formerly the National Capital Region) with the National Park Service (NPS). In this position she is responsible for hundreds of park areas in the District of Columbia, and parts of Maryland, Virginia, and West Virginia, an approximately $350 million budget and more than 1,000 employees. The area's nearly 65,000 acres of federal parkland include some of the most famous places in the world, such as the White House, Antietam National Battlefield and the Jefferson Memorial. The area also includes parks beloved by residents such as Rock Creek Park, Anacostia Park and Catoctin Mountain Park. More than 54 million visitors travel to the area's national parks for recreation, education, inspiration and relaxation each year. Kym is a 34-year veteran of the National Park Service and joined the National Capital Area from Colonial National Historical Park where she had served as Superintendent since 2015. Previous positions include Deputy Superintendent of Glacier National Park, and acting Superintendent at a number of parks including Glen Canyon National Recreation Area, Glacier National Park, the Outer Banks Group of National Parks, and Fort Monroe National Monument. She began her NPS career at Olympic National Park holding a variety of clerical, dispatcher and paralegal positions. She has also served in the NPS Washington Headquarters Office where she worked on publishing regulations for national parks, and later held the position of Special Assistant to the Assistant Secretary for Fish and Wildlife and Parks. Kym graduated from the Harvard University, Kennedy School of Government Senior Executive Fellows program in 2014 and the Department of the Interior Senior Executive Service Candidate Development Program in 2016. She is the proud mother of three sons; 24, 19, and 12.

John Stanwich, National Park Service Liaison to the White House

John Stanwich serves as the National Park Service Liaison to the White House overseeing the National Park Service’s responsibilities for the care of the White House, it’s grounds, and the park areas immediately surrounding the over two century old structure in the Nation’s Capital. Stanwich has over 25 years of federal government experience. Stanwich served as one of the NPS leads for the 56th – 59th Presidential Inaugurations. During his previous positions as Deputy NPS Liaison to the White House and Manager of President’s Park, he has participated in the planning and implementation of nationally significant events such as the National Christmas Tree Lighting, the White House Easter Egg Roll, and numerous State Arrival Ceremonies. Stanwich’s duties have also included overseeing the White House Visitor Center, care and preservation for several monuments, memorials, statues, and gardens and the monitoring of First Amendment demonstrations that frequently occurred in Lafayette Park and on the White House sidewalk on Pennsylvania Avenue.

A native of Virginia, John graduated from Boston University, and received a Master of Science in International Relations from the London School of Economics, and pursued Ph.D. studies in American Diplomatic History at the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy.
