JAMES RUSHTON EARLY LEARNING CENTER TOUR BACKGROUND

The Woodlawn Foundation was created in 2010 with the mission to break to cycle of poverty and revitalize Woodlawn into a vibrant and sustainable community. The foundation is locally known as “the community quarterback” and follows the Purpose Built Communities revitalization model, which focuses on housing, education, and community wellness.

The James Rushton Early Learning Center is a child care center and preschool developed by the Woodlawn Foundation and the James Rushton I Foundation. The center occupies a renovated former bank branch and opened in April 2017.

TOUR PARTICIPANT BIOS

Randall Woodfin, Mayor of Birmingham (D): Randall Woodfin is the 34th and current mayor of Birmingham, Alabama. He previously served as the President of the Birmingham City School Board from 2013-2015 and as an Assistant City Attorney for the City of Birmingham from 2009–2017. Woodfin is a graduate from Morehouse College and Samford University Cumberland School of Law.

Congresswoman Terri Sewell (D, AL-7): Sewell is the representative for Alabama’s 7th congressional district. She has served since Jan 5, 2011.

A native of Selma, Sewell is a graduate of Princeton University, Harvard Law School, and Oxford University. Before entering politics, she had been a securities lawyer for Davis Polk & Wardwell and a public finance lawyer for Maynard, Cooper & Gale, where she was the first Black woman to make partner. She is the first African-American woman to have been elected to Congress from Alabama.

Mashonda Taylor, Executive Director, Woodlawn Foundation: Mashonda Taylor was named Executive Director of the Woodlawn Foundation in July 2020. She has worked at the Foundation since 2012 and served in various roles, including as office administrator, programming officer and chief operating officer. Taylor is a Birmingham native with degrees from Loyola University and Gonzaga University. Taylor is also a member of the executive committee of Jones Valley Teaching Farm and chairs the Diversity & Inclusion Taskforce for the Junior League of Birmingham, where she also served as a board member in 2017.

Delyne Hicks Executive Director, James Rushton Early Learning Center: Delyne Hicks is the Executive Director of the James Rushton Early Learning in Woodlawn, Alabama. She has served as a classroom teacher, public school administrator, college instructor, and non-profit administrator for over 30 years. She has several publications that focus on curriculum and instruction. She received her BS and MA from the University of Alabama at Birmingham and has completed all the coursework for a Ph.D.

Lakiera Christian,Teacher, James Rushton Early Learning Center: Lakiera Christian has been an early childhood teacher for 5 years. She grew up in the Woodlawn community. Lakiera realized she had a passion to help children when she was in elementary school, when she would regularly read to younger children.

Elizabeth Hodge, Teacher, James Rushton Early Learning Center: Elizabeth Hodge has 17 years of teaching experience in early childhood. She attended Birmingham City Schools and graduated from Phillips High School in 1991. She attended Lawson State Community College and obtained a Child Development Associate (CDA). Elizabeth has three children.

Catherine Israel, Teacher, James Rushton Early Learning Center: Catherine Israel is 25 years old and originally from Wedowee, Alabama. She graduated from Randolph County High School in 2014, and moved to Birmingham to attend UAB five years ago. She found her passion for teaching young children while working in Australia during a gap year. Since then, she has been working in Child Development, primarily with children ages 3-5. She is currently in school at Jefferson State Community College obtaining her associate’s degree in Child Development.
